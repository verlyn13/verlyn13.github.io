Publications

Selected peer-reviewed publications in mathematics, AI foundations, and autonomous systems.

2024

Mathematical Frameworks for Agent Coordination in Autonomous Systems

Johnson, J.

Journal of Applied Mathematics, 45(3), 234-251.

This paper presents a category-theoretic framework for understanding and implementing coordination mechanisms in multi-agent autonomous systems. We demonstrate that agent interactions can be modeled as morphisms in a suitable category, providing both theoretical insights and practical implementation strategies.

Formal Verification of Tool Integration Protocols

Johnson, J., Chen, L., & Martinez, R.

Proceedings of the International Conference on Autonomous Systems, pp. 156-171.

We present a formal method for verifying the correctness of tool integration protocols in AI systems. Our approach uses temporal logic to specify integration requirements and model checking to verify implementation compliance.

2023

Category-Theoretic Approaches to Multi-Agent Systems

Johnson, J. & Smith, A.

Advances in AI Research, 12(2), 89-104.

We explore the application of category theory to the design and analysis of multi-agent systems, showing how functorial relationships between agent categories can guide system architecture decisions.

Optimization Methods for Distributed Agent Coordination

Johnson, J., Park, S., & Williams, D.

Mathematical Programming, 189(1), 45-72.

This work develops new optimization algorithms for coordinating distributed agents under communication constraints. We prove convergence guarantees and demonstrate performance on benchmark problems.

2022

Formal Verification Methods for Autonomous Tool Integration

Johnson, J.

Mathematical Structures in Computer Science, 32(7), 445-468.

We present a comprehensive framework for formally verifying the correctness of autonomous tool integration systems, with applications to AI-driven development environments.

Graph-Theoretic Models of Agent Communication Networks

Johnson, J. & Lee, K.

Networks, 79(4), 512-531.

This paper develops graph-theoretic models for analyzing communication patterns in multi-agent systems, with emphasis on robustness and efficiency metrics.
