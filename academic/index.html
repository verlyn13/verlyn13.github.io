Academic Philosophy

My academic journey bridges pure mathematics and practical computation. Through teaching, I've discovered that the most profound mathematical insights often emerge from attempting to build working systems. This perspective drives both my research and pedagogical approach.

In the classroom, I emphasize understanding through implementation. Students don't just learn algorithms—they build them, debug them, and discover their limitations. This hands-on approach has proven especially effective in preparing students for the intersection of mathematics and modern AI systems.
